Average Distance Traveled

I calculated average distanced traveled by estimating that the average person rides a bike at a pace of 15km/hr and then converting that into 4.2 m/s and multiplying it by the duration of a ride and finding the mean of all trips

Commuter Data:

I estimated that the number of commuters was around 494/2 = 247 people. I got this number by taking average of all the trips done in a day. This would show, on average, the number of unique trips a day. This is significant because a person who was commuting to work would likely commute to work on a daily basis and do around two trips a day (one from and to work). In addition, commuters go every day to work, thus the average number of bike share trips will be the closest to the number of people consistently using bikeshare for commuting. Thus, the average number of trips divide by 2 will give you the closest number of commuters that consistently uses bike share to go to work.

Season Data:

I graphed all the trips on a bar graph with the y axis being the number of trips done in that specific month. By doing this, I discovered that late summer, early fall (08-10) were the most popular season with a peak on August. After August, there is a strong decrease in trips for the winter season (12 - 03).

Trip Route Category-Passholder Data:

From the table it is clear that the most popular combination by a huge margin were Walk-up, One-Way and Montly Pass, One-way. One way is much more popular then roundtrips. One way-Monthly is by far the most popular for people commuting to and from work because they must park their bike to work and then get another bike to go home every day. Furthermore, montly users would be more inclined to use the bikes because they had already paid for it. In addition, for typical users if they wanted to get from one destination to another with the bike they would more often then not simply be going one-way which explains why Walk Up - One way combination was so popular. The way people were using the bike would make one combination much more popular then the other. Since people were using one-way more then the roundtrip, we can assume that people were using the bike share as a way to get around LA. If they weren't trying to get around the City then roundtrip would be much more popular since that would indicate people were not trying to get to any destination with the bikes and were using it to exercise or other recreational use rather than as a mode of transportation.
